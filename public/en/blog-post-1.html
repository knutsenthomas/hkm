How to Keep Faith in a Busy Everyday Life

05 Feb, 2024 Teaching 5 min read

In today's hectic society, it can be challenging to maintain a close relationship with God. We explore practical tips and biblical principles to keep faith in the midst of a busy everyday life.

The Challenge of a Busy Life

Many of us find our days full of commitments - work, family, friends, and countless other activities. In this chaos, it can be easy to forget the most important thing: our relationship with God. But the Bible reminds us to seek Him first (Matt 6:33).

Practical Tips to Keep the Faith

1. Start the Day with God

Although it may be tempting to jump straight out of bed and into the day's tasks, it is important to prioritize time with God in the morning. This can be as simple as 10-15 minutes of prayer and Bible reading. "But those who wait on the Lord shall renew their strength" (Isa 40:31).

2. Prayer Throughout the Day

Prayer doesn't always have to be long, formal moments. We can pray short prayers throughout the day - on the way to work, during lunch break, or while doing chores. "Pray without ceasing" (1 Thess 5:17).

3. Fellowship with Other Christians

It is important not to isolate oneself. Being part of a Christian community gives us support, encouragement, and accountability. "Let us not neglect our meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing" (Heb 10:25).

4. Prioritize What Matters Most

Sometimes we have to say no to good things to say yes to the best things. Evaluate your priorities and ensure that your spiritual growth is not prioritized down.

God's Grace is Sufficient

The most important thing to remember is that our relationship with God is not based on our own efforts, but on His grace. Even when we fail, He is faithful. "My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness" (2 Cor 12:9).
